Help users to

Access a service with identity documents

Also known as authenticate or identifying users

EXPERIMENTAL

This pattern is currently experimental because more research is needed to validate it.

Let users access a service in a secure way by authenticating themselves with their identity documents.

When to use this pattern

Follow this pattern if you are helping users access a service by authenticating users with an identity document. It could be the same document they’ve used to apply previously, the document your service has on record for them or the document that’s attached to their account.

Services using this pattern

  • Access UK
  • Apply for the EU Settlement Scheme
  • Status: right to rent, right to work, view & prove
  • Update your EU Settlement Scheme details

Research

More research is needed on this pattern.

In research, it has been shown to be confusing to users which document they should select in order to verify themselves. For example, they may have used the update your details service and not understand that they will now need to use these new details, rather than the original document they may have used to previously authenticate themselves.

When using the biometric identity permit and card as one of the identifying documents, research has shown that there is the potential for confusion over which one to choose. Users tend to get confused about which document they have.

Further research is needed on this. If you've used this pattern in your service, please get in touch to share your research findings.

Accessibility

If your service uses this pattern, we’d be interested to hear any insights you have on any accessibility considerations.

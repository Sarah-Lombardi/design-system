Nationality
Experimental

Ask users for their nationality.

When to use this pattern

Follow this pattern when asking a user for their nationality as part of a service. Only ask if you need it to deliver a service. Common scenarios when a user may be asked for their nationality:

  • Apply for an identity document, such as a passport
  • Apply for travel permits such as visas
  • Access a previous application
  • Update their details

How to use it

Make it clear:

  • Why you’re asking for a user’s nationality, if for example you’re asking as part of a particular application journey
  • Whose nationality you’re asking for, for example, if your user is filling out information about a relative or guardian

Use the GOV.UK country and territory autocomplete with this pattern, and depending on the context, allow users to enter:

  • Several forms of citizenship. So for example, Britain, Scotland, Northern Ireland for a national of the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland
  • Entities that aren’t recognized as countries by the UK, for example Taiwan and Hong Kong

Examples

Ask users for:

  • — their nationality
  • — the country on their passport
  • — their country of birth
  • — all the nationalities they hold
  • — a nationality out of a select list

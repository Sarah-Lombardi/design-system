Help users to

Access a service

Also known as: authentication

Let users access a service in a secure way by authenticating themselves.

Overview

Most one-time services won't need to authenticate their users. When a service does need to verify a user’s identity, it needs to balance ease of access with keeping a service and its user data secure. You should think about this early on when designing your service.

You might want to authenticate users if:

  • your service allows users to save and complete a transaction at another time
  • a user can check the status of a transaction

Authentication is not the same as identity assurance

Identity assurance confirms the true identity of a user. The checks are usually done before confirming a user's suitability for something. Identity assurance can consist of:

  • confirming the user is human
  • confirming the validity of information and documents
  • checking for fraud
  • checking for warnings about a user

Variations

There are several ways that you can help users to access a service securely:

  • — with identity documents
  • — with personal details
  • — with reference numbers
  • — with security codes (two-factor authentication)

You can also authenticate users using GOV.UK Verify.

Don’t use National Insurance numbers to verify a user’s identity. You can find out more about National Insurance numbers and fraud in the service manual.

You should consult a Home Office security specialist as they can advise you on the type of authentication that’s right for your service.
